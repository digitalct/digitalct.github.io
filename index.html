Magneto-Optic Kerr Effect (MOKE) Demonstration

Exploring the interaction of light and magnetism with various magnet configurations

Introduction to Magneto-Optic Kerr Effect

The Magneto-Optic Kerr Effect (MOKE) is a phenomenon where the polarization of light is rotated upon reflection from a magnetized surface. It is used to study magnetic properties of materials. There are three main configurations: Polar, Longitudinal, and Transverse, each corresponding to different orientations of the magnetic field relative to the surface and the plane of incidence.

Single Pole Electromagnet

A single pole electromagnet, often a solenoid, generates a uniform magnetic field suitable for demonstrating the basic working of the MOKE. It can be used to apply a field perpendicular or parallel to the sample surface depending on orientation.

Double Pole Magnets for Longitudinal and Transverse Effects

Double pole magnets (dipole configuration) are used to create in-plane magnetic fields. For the longitudinal Kerr effect, the field is parallel to the plane of incidence, affecting the polarization differently than in the transverse configuration, where the field is perpendicular to the incidence plane.

Quadrupole Magnets for Polar Effects

Quadrupole magnets provide a more complex field configuration, often used for focusing or precise control in magnetic setups. In MOKE, they can enhance the polar effect by creating strong perpendicular fields with minimal stray fields.
